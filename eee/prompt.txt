### STRICT COMPILATION & DRAWING RULES (TO AVOID COMPILE ERRORS)
1. ONLY USE COORDINATE-BASED DRAWING: 
   Always specify exact (X,Y) coordinates for every path.
   CORRECT: \\draw (0,0) to[R] (2,0);
   INCORRECT: \\draw (0,0) to[R] ++(2,0);  // Avoid relative positioning as it leads to overlapping and errors.
2. ORTHOGONAL ROUTING ONLY:
   Make 90-degree orthogonal connections wherever possible.
   CORRECT: \\draw (0,0) |- (2,2); 
   INCORRECT: \\draw (0,0) -- (2,2); // Diagonal wires often look bad or intersection fails. 
3. PROPER SPACING:
   Keep at least 2 coordinate units of horizontal space between components (e.g., (0,0) to (2,0)).
   Keep at least 2 units of vertical space between parallel branches.
4. LABELS AND VALUES:
   Use `l=` to apply component values directly. Ensure braces `{}` protect equal signs: `l=$R_1{=}10k\\Omega$`. 
   If a label overlaps, use `l_=` (below) or `l^=` (above) to position it to prevent overlapping.
5. GROUND NODES:
   Always terminate ground paths with an empty node: `node[ground]{}`. Do NOT forget the `{}` bracket.
   CORRECT: \\draw (0,0) node[ground]{};
   INCORRECT: \\draw (0,0) node[ground];
6. COMPLEX COMPONENTS (OP-AMPS & TRANSISTORS):
   Always define them at absolute coordinates using `\\node`, then draw paths from their anchors.
   EXAMPLE: \\node[op amp] (U1) at (5,2) {}; \\draw (U1.out) to[R] (8,2);
7. INTERSECTION DOTS:
   When wires connect, use `to[short, -*]` or explicitly use a node `node[circle,fill,inner sep=1pt]{}` so it is visually clear they connect.
